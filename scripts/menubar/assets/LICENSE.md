# Menu-bar badge tool-mark assets

`claude-mark.png` and `codex-mark.png` are **original, self-authored neutral
placeholder marks** — a monochrome diamond and a monochrome triangle, the same two
neutral silhouettes the badge draws as text (`◆` / `▲`). They are generated as small
(26×26, ~13px @2x) transparent RGBA **template images** so SwiftBar's `templateImage=`
tints them to the menu-bar foreground color.

## They are NOT the real product logos

They are **placeholders**, not the Claude or OpenAI/Codex brand marks. The badge's
"Tool marks → Logos" option is **opt-in and off by default**, and the neutral text
glyph (`◆` / `▲`) is **always the floor** — the badge's honesty and state reading
never depend on an image rendering (xbar, or a failed/absent image, still names the
tool). If you want the badge to show the *real* product logos, drop the actual brand
marks in here (same filenames, small monochrome transparent PNGs) — but that is a
**trademark/licensing decision for you to make**. The nominative-fair-use posture the
design ratified (small, monochrome, opt-in, no endorsement implied, a guaranteed
neutral fallback) is the product decision; sourcing a cleanly-licensed real asset is
left to the operator. llmdash ships only the neutral placeholders so the repo carries
no third-party brand asset.
